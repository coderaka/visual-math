What am I looking at?

The Total Variation distance between two probability distributions $P$ and $Q$ is defined as:

Geometrically, this is exactly half the shaded area between the two density curves. The cyan region shows where $p(x) > q(x)$, and the magenta region shows where $q(x) > p(x)$. These two regions always have equal area — each equals $d_{\TV}(P, Q)$.
